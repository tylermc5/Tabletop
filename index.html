The problem

Why your last Instagram ad didn't actually work.

Restaurants spend on Yelp, Instagram boosts, third-party delivery promos, and one-off creator deals — and most of it underperforms. The reason is simple: consumers don't trust ads. They trust people.

And not just any people. They trust local creators whose dinner recommendations they already follow. Almost a third of US consumers have tried a new restaurant in the last year because a creator told them to.

But cracking creator marketing yourself is brutal. Sourcing creators in your city. Negotiating per-post rates. Writing briefs. Chasing deliverables. Measuring what actually worked. Most restaurants try it once, get a single disappointing post for $800, and quit.

That's not creator marketing. That's a wasted afternoon.

What we look for in Tabletop creators.

Hyperlocal focus

You post food content in a specific city or region. Hyperlocal creators win on this platform. National food creators are great for cookbook deals; they don't drive foot traffic.

Engaged audience

We look at engagement rate, save rate, and how often your comments include "trying this!" — not follower count. Strong micro creators (10K–100K) typically outperform big accounts.

Craft

Quality matters. Lighting, sound, narrative. We're not asking for high production value — authenticity beats production every time — but we are asking for craft.

Long-term mindset

Tabletop only works if creators stay. We don't do one-off campaigns. If you're shopping for the highest single-post rate, we're not your platform.

What we don't do

  • Negotiate you down to nothing on rate
  • Send 12-page contracts
  • Ask for "rights in perpetuity"
  • Use your content for ads without paying you
  • Send payment late, ever
  • Match you with brands you wouldn't pick yourself
